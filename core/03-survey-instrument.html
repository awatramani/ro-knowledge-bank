The Instrument

Understanding the Questionnaire You Carry

The questionnaire is not a form — it is the measurement tool for the study. Every word is deliberate. Every skip pattern has a reason. Before you go to the field, you must know it the way a carpenter knows their tools.

Surveyor asks:

"The question seems obvious. Can I just paraphrase it to save time?"

Senior RA replies:

"No. The exact wording is the measurement. If you change the words, you change what is being measured. Two surveyors asking the same question differently create noise that researchers cannot correct for. Read every question exactly as written."

📋 Modules

The questionnaire is divided into modules, each covering a topic area. Read all modules before Day 1. Know which households get which modules.

⏱ Timing

Know the expected interview duration. If you are consistently taking 40 minutes more than expected, a question or skip is being applied incorrectly.

🔀 Skip Logic

Skip patterns route respondents through relevant questions. An incorrect skip creates missing data or contradictory responses — the most common data quality failure.

🔑 The Three Things You Must Know Cold

(1) The respondent eligibility criteria — who exactly qualifies for this interview. (2) The skip conditions — what triggers a skip and where it leads. (3) The sensitive question protocol — which questions require extra care, preambles, or privacy measures.

Instrument Scenario: The Household Size MismatchYou are recording a household roster. The respondent says 7 members live in the household. When you complete the roster, you have only 5 names. SurveyCTO flags a mismatch. Wrong response: Change the household count to 5 to clear the flag. Right response: Probe gently. Ask who else lives here. Re-confirm with the respondent. If the count stays at 5 after probing, record 5 and write a note explaining the discrepancy. Never change data to satisfy a validation — investigate first.
⚠️ Instrument Red Lines
  • Reading question text from memory instead of the screen
  • Skipping consent module because "they already agreed last time"
  • Entering "Don't Know" to rush past a question you haven't asked
  • Accepting the first answer to a sensitive question without allowing reflection time
  • Submitting a form with a note that says "will fix later"
Sensitive Questions

Handling Difficult and Sensitive Modules

Some modules cover income, debt, domestic violence, mental health, and personal losses. The preambles are designed by ethicists. Read them exactly. Do not improvise.

💰 Income & Assets

Never signal surprise or judgment at reported amounts. The farmer who says he earns ₹3,000/month and the one who says ₹3 lakh/month both deserve a neutral face and the same follow-up.

🏥 Health & Mortality

When asking about child deaths or illness, pause after sensitive answers. Offer water. Ask if they want to continue. Never rush a respondent through grief.

⚡ Domestic Violence

Gender violence modules require private, one-on-one settings. Never with another household member present. Know the safeguarding referral protocol before asking these questions.

🔑 The Preamble Is Not Optional

Every sensitive module opens with a preamble that frames the questions, establishes confidentiality, and signals that there are no right or wrong answers. Skipping the preamble is not saving time — it is removing the ethical protection the study team designed for the respondent.

Quality in Questioning

Real-Time Interview Quality

The best instrument in the world produces bad data if the interview is conducted poorly. Your technique is the last line of defence.

Weak Interview Technique
  • Reading the next question before the respondent has finished answering
  • Suggesting an answer when the respondent pauses ("Is it maybe around 5?")
  • Skipping probes because you think you understand what they meant
  • Rushing through the closing because your next appointment is near
Strong Interview Technique
  • Waiting a full 3 seconds after a response before moving on
  • Probing with neutral phrases: "Can you tell me more?" "Can you be more specific?"
  • Recording exact responses, even if they seem inconsistent with earlier answers
  • Closing every interview with a genuine thank-you and a clear explanation of next steps
